Acoustic guitars are portable and allow for great instrumentation. There is a variety of songs you can play on Acoustic guitar and each with different styles. Acoustic guitars are fairly simple, when you pluck one of the 6 strings, the sound will travel through the guitar. The shape and volume of the guitar allows that sound to be amplified naturally.

Electric guitars are a great instrument when playing for large crowds and for more volume control. They require an amplifier connected to a speaker for the electric guitar sound. Electric guitars work nearly the same as an Acoustic guitar but the metal strings that will react with the “pickups” sending a signal to the amplifier and speaker when vibrated. These pickups are magnets wrapped in thousands of coils of copper wire that will sense a disruption of the magnetic field when the metallic string directly above the pickup vibrates. When the disruption is sensed by the pickups, the amplifier will amplify the signal being sent to the speaker.


History of the Guitar

After World War II, the guitar became one of the world's most popular musical instruments. Advances in manufacturing made guitars more affordable and widely available. The rise of rock and roll in the 1950s brought the electric guitar into the spotlight, with musicians such as Chuck Berry inspiring young people to learn the instrument. During the 1960s and 1970s, rock, folk, country, blues, and pop music all featured the guitar as a central instrument. As these styles spread through radio, television, and recordings, millions of people around the world began playing the guitar. Today, it remains one of the most popular instruments because it is portable, versatile, and used in nearly every style of music.
